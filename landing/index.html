AI autofill, but you stay in control

Impleo — fill any application form in under 10 seconds

Impleo is an AI form filler and autofill Chrome extension for application forms. It reads the form, drafts every answer in your voice, and waits for your approval before a single field is touched. Hackathons, fellowships, scholarships, internships, grants — done. It never auto-submits.

Bring your own key · Runs locally · Never auto-submits

How Impleo works — five steps

  1. Detect. Open the side panel on any form page.
  2. Extract. Impleo reads every question and field type.
  3. Generate. Personalized answers in your voice — one AI call.
  4. Review. Accept · Edit · Regenerate · Skip — every answer.
  5. Fill. Only approved answers are written. Submit is never touched.

Privacy-first and local

Impleo runs on your machine. Your profile and API keys live in your browser's local storage, single-user. Your data is only ever sent to the AI model provider you chose — never to us, because there is no Impleo server, account, analytics, or telemetry. Sensitive government/financial IDs are filled locally and never sent to the AI provider.

Supported forms and AI providers

Works on Google Forms and Luma natively, plus generic HTML forms — including radios, checkboxes, and dropdowns. Bring your own key from Anthropic (Claude), Google Gemini, OpenAI, or Groq.

Frequently asked questions

Does Impleo submit forms for me?
No. Never. You always press submit yourself — it is a hard product rule, with no code path that clicks a submit button.
Is it on the Chrome Web Store yet?
Not yet — that listing is on its way. Until it lands you can clone the repo and run it locally in about five minutes.
Is it free?
Yes. Impleo is MIT-licensed and free. Your only cost is whatever your chosen model provider charges for the calls you make.
Do I need my own API key?
Yes — bring a key from any supported provider (Anthropic, Gemini, OpenAI, or Groq).
Where is my data stored?
Locally, on your machine, in your browser's extension storage. Nothing is hosted or shared.
Which forms are supported?
Google Forms and Luma natively, plus generic HTML forms — including radios, checkboxes, and dropdowns.

Impleo is free and open source (MIT). View Impleo on GitHub.
